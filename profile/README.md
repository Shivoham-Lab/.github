## What is Shivoham Lab?

> Shivoham means "I am pure consciousness." This lab is an extension of that - a space to think clearly, build honestly, and ship things that matter. It is indie. It is early. And that is a feature.

---

## Core Principles

### No hierarchy. Seriously.
There is no "senior" or "junior" here. Everyone ships, everyone has opinions, everyone matters equally. If an idea is good, it is good regardless of who said it.

### You come first.
Exams, internships, health, life - these always take priority. Nobody will chase you for a deadline. If you need to step back, just say so. No guilt, no drama. The lab will still be here.

### Async by default.
No mandatory meetings, no standups, no check-ins. Work when you work best. Communication happens on Discord/Telegram — and responses happen when they happen. Urgency is the exception, not the norm.

### Everything is in the open.
Our code is public. Our process is public. We build in public, we fail in public, we iterate in public. No gatekeeping. If you are building something at the lab, you own your contributions — they are yours forever.

---

## Contribution Rhythm — How We Work

We run on **seasons** — not deadlines. Each season is ~4 months. There is one focus project per season. Small tools ship whenever they are ready.

| Cadence | What |
|---|---|
| Per season (~4 months) | 1 focus project — one flagship or a major update to an existing one. A direction to work toward, not a contract. |
| Anytime | Small tools — useful, scoped utilities that solve a real problem. No quota. Ship when ready. |
| Always | Research & ideas — exploration counts as work. Writing a doc, testing a theory, filing an issue — all of it moves us forward. |

> If a season ends and the focus project is not done — that is fine. We carry it forward. We do not rush to hit an arbitrary line.

## The Rules (there are not many)

### Do
- Ship something, even if it is small.
- Say when you are blocked.
- Ask dumb questions and trush me they are never dumb.
- Disagree openly.
- Credit your teammates.

### Do not
- Ghost without a word.
- Push broken code to main without a note.
- Gatekeep knowledge.
- Act like your time is more valuable than someone else's.

### On code
- Write readable code over clever code.
- Comment when the why is not obvious.
- PR descriptions matter write them for the person reviewing, not for yourself.


## Build With Us

We build in public, but not everything starts public.

If you want to cobuild a project with the lab, you get early access before launch. That means you can ship it to a client, sell it, or use it however you need  before it ever goes open source.

You build with us. You get first rights. We launch publicly after.

**→ [Talk to us](mailto:shivoham.s27@gmail.com)**

### On feedback
- Feedback is about the work, never the person.
- Give it clearly. Receive it without ego.
- "This does not work because X" is not an attack it is a gift.

---

## What "Active" Means Here

Active does not mean online every day. It means: when you say you will do something, you do it or you flag early that you cannot. That is it. One honest message beats two weeks of silence.

---

## The Spirit of the Lab

We are open source. Nobody here is obligated. The only reason to show up is because you want to build something real.

This is not a company. There are no performance reviews, no KPIs, no hierarchy. Just people who think deeply and want to ship things that matter.
